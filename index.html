Clouding Around

"Give me a virtualized infrastructure and a cloud platform on which to run it, and I shall move the world" - Archimedes (paraphrased)

I have to admit that my journey out of the server room and into the Cloud at first felt like capitulation, rather than ascension. I was an old-school, baremetal rack-and-stack sysadmin. For far too many years I dismissed the great, looming Cloud as little more than someone else's datacenter, another layer of obfuscation between me and the machine. Infrastructure-as-a-Service? Private Cloud? Weren't these phrases oxymoronic? I was mired by monolithic thinking.

Thankfully, experience has taught me that tales of such hubris rarely end well for the hubristic, and also to (eventually) recognize a good thing when I see it. When it comes to ease of use, deployment speed, scalability, and administrative overhead (or rather, lack thereof), the Cloud is indomitable. The revolution had already been virtualized. And it was time for me to level up to the challenge.

And so, with an S3 Bucket in hand and an eye open for elegant tech, I've packed up my CAT6, tossed my Twinax and am joining the on-demand, highly-available, super-scalable, serverless revolution. Cloud solutions are agile, performant, durable, and accessible--they can provide tremendous value to your organization... just like me. Cloud ho!

My face is like this

My passion for computing was ignited through the glowing glass of a pixelated tube display and to the classic clacking sounds of a venerable Model M mechanical keyboard. It was the early 90’s and I had just set up my first computer, an IBM PS/2—complete with a stack of floppy disks that actually had some flop to ‘em.

It was a relic even then, but I was hooked. This little machine, capable of displaying just 16 colors at a time, seemed to offer endless possibilties. In rapid succession I would upgrade to faster machines, and upgrade those to be even faster yet. A miserably slow dial-up modem promised a seemingly infinite reach across the vast yet nascent web of cyberspace, at least until you got booted by an incoming call from Grandma.

Thinking back on it, perhaps it was the dulcet tone of that 2800baud beast shaking hands with the great unknown (or AOL, more likely), that drew me into the networking field in the first place. Ah, memories.

Back in the present, I’ve been busy leveraging my experience as a network engineer and computer-nerd-in-general to expand my skillset to meet the exploding needs of today’s cloud-connected world. That sounds good, right? Working through Full Stack open 2020 (https:///fullstackopen.com) and AWS' Devops Engineer course I’ve learned to develop simple, responsive web apps in React, and then to provision, orchestrate, deploy and manage them in the cloud. Along the way of this core path, I’m exploring other frameworks and solutions that I come across. My new favorite Vercel, which I used to launch this site. It allows the quickest, most automated and seamless deployments to the cloud I’ve seen yet.

When I’m not leveling up my nerd skills, I tend to enjoy significantly less high-tech hobbies. I love to hike with my two canine companions, an old rescue poodle and a boisterous black lab. My kitchen is my workshop where I whip up from-scratch everythings, bake bread, roast coffee, and occasionally brew up a beer. I have a small garden and try (but never succeed) to keep my pantry full of home canned goods.
